**Alternated AI**

Evolution shows up anywhere solutions are being selected.

Biology is the oldest example. Organisms reproduce, mutations appear, and the environment filters out what doesn’t work. Death and generational turnover are crucial. Without them, populations quickly lose their ability to adapt.

Automotive engineering follows a similar pattern, just slower and with humans steering the wheel. Manufacturers design new models, the market stress-tests them in the real world, and sales act as the selection mechanism. Inefficient designs disappear, while the best ideas gradually become industry standards.

AI pushes this process into overdrive. Models are trained, benchmarked, and replaced by newer versions at a relentless pace.

Biology evolves through generations.
Technology evolves through product versions.
AI can evolve almost in real time.

Is death inevitable?
Is mortality the guarantee of evolution?
Is evolution the guarantee of progress?
And if that’s true, do we want AI to be mortal too?
If so, the real question becomes practical:
how could mortality be built into AI systems?
