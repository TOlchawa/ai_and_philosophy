**A quick retrospective**

I remember a time when I seriously wondered whether something like YouTube would ever take off.

It felt like:
- hardly anyone had a camera
- uploading video to the internet was a hassle
- and it all seemed more like a niche than something that could become normal

Reality proved that wrong — fast.

---

Now I catch myself having a similar thought, just in a different space.

I’m wondering whether the AI + robotics wave will actually go mainstream.

Because I see the same kind of friction:
- integrating AI with hardware isn’t trivial
- cost and reliability still matter
- and the “last mile” in the physical world is much harder than in software

---

And yet…

Back then:
- cameras ended up in every phone
- uploading became a one-click action
- and the platform handled the rest

Today:
- AI models are getting cheaper and easier to use
- hardware is becoming more accessible
- and integration is slowly moving out of expert-only territory

---

It’s not the same thing.

But the pattern feels familiar.

First:
"this is too complicated, and nobody really needs it"

Then:
"how did we ever live without this?"

---

Curious whether in a few years we’ll look at AI-powered robotics the same way we now look at online video.
