**The Art of Discovery**

I tried to isolate the core conditions behind successful work on new ideas.

Below is the human version. In parentheses, I added a rough functional analog for AI — not a literal equivalent, but the system requirement that plays a similar role if we treat AI as a researcher.

- A minimum level of material stability
 (for AI: stable access to compute, memory, storage, and permissions)

- Long, uninterrupted stretches of focus
 (for AI: long-running inference, training, or search without resets, truncation, or context collapse)

- Strong directional pressure
 (for humans: ambition, obsession, conflict, a sense of mission; for AI: a hard objective function, optimization pressure, and a real research target)

- Access to tools, materials, and knowledge
 (for AI: papers, datasets, code, simulators, instruments, and search)

- Partial isolation from noise
 (for AI: filtering junk data, prompt clutter, irrelevant retrieval, and low-quality feedback)

- Contact with existing ideas
 (for AI: retrieval, ranking, and filtering over prior literature, models, and experiment logs)

- External support
 (for AI: an orchestrator, supervisor, lab infrastructure, funding, or a broader agent/tool ecosystem)

- Tolerance for uncertainty
 (for AI: keeping multiple hypotheses alive under incomplete information)

- Contact with reality
 (for AI: experimental feedback, simulation results, benchmark outcomes, or expert validation)

- Long-horizon continuity
 (for AI: persistent memory, reproducible workflows, cumulative experiment tracking, and state retention)

The formula is probably still close to this:

minimum stability + maximum focus + a real problem to solve + feedback from reality
