**The Engineer Who Stopped Designing**

Once there was an engineer who tried to design the perfect solution.

He planned everything.
Architecture diagrams.
Optimization strategies.
Edge cases.
Weeks passed.
The system still didn’t work.

So he tried something different.
Instead of designing the perfect solution,
he created a thousand imperfect ones.
Most of them failed instantly.
Some worked a little.
A few worked surprisingly well.
He kept the best ones.
Then he mutated them.
Combined them.
Tested them again.

After many generations, something emerged
that he could never have designed directly.

Not because he lacked intelligence.
But because the search space was too large for reasoning alone.
That’s when he realized something simple:
Nature doesn’t design.
Nature searches.

And sometimes the smartest thing an engineer can do
is stop pretending he can see the whole solution
— and let evolution explore it instead.
