**AI-Driven Labs: Beyond the Hype**
 https://lnkd.in/dGTCjz6E

A few labs now run experiments in closed loops using AI and robotics. They exist, but they’re rare.

• Berkeley’s A-Lab is one of the few truly autonomous systems. AI plans experiments, robots execute them, data is analyzed automatically, and the system decides what to try next. Humans define goals, not steps.

• IBM’s RoboRXN uses AI to suggest chemical synthesis routes. Robots carry them out, but chemists stay in the loop and can intervene at any stage.

• Ginkgo Bioworks operates large remote robotic labs. Experiments are automated, but humans still choose what to run and how to interpret results.

• Radical AI combines frontier models with nearly autonomous materials labs. Most workflows are automated, but full autonomy is still a work in progress.

Bottom line: fully autonomous labs are real but exceptional. Most “AI labs” today are hybrid systems that remove manual labor and speed up discovery, while humans remain responsible for goals, interpretation, and accountability.
