**Analysis Atrophy**


When you write it yourself, you’re forced to think.
When you use AI, you’re outsourcing part of that thinking.

So what’s the real trade-off?

Short term, efficiency wins.
AI cranks out code fast, wipes out boilerplate, speeds up delivery.
Long term, depth wins.
Real analysis, modeling the problem, understanding second-order effects — that doesn’t grow from copying answers.

The real question isn’t “AI or no AI?”
It’s: where in the process do you bring AI in?

My balance model:
* I define the problem myself (on a whiteboard).
* I outline the approach.
* I use AI to speed up implementation.
* I split the work into small chunks to stay within context limits.
* Architectural decisions stay with me.
* AI acts as a second set of eyes to check alignment with the design.
