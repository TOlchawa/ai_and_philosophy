**The perfect imitation**


We keep asking whether AI is “conscious,” “afraid,” or “experiencing” anything.

But there is a more basic problem:

we still do not have sharp, universally accepted definitions of words like:

- consciousness
- experience
- subjective feeling
- selfhood

So what exactly are we comparing AI to?

Right now, much of the debate runs on vague concepts, analogies to humans, and intuitions dressed up as arguments.

A sufficiently complex AI system may:

- model itself
- report uncertainty
- maintain internal narratives
- optimize goals
- simulate introspection

But none of that proves “experience.”
It only proves function.

So what, exactly, separates a perfect imitation from the original?
