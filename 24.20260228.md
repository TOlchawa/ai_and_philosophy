**Sometimes less is more**

https://lnkd.in/dHCH8F9z

Too much can wreck even the best system. When you’re working with AI, an overly restrictive spec doesn’t improve quality — it often drags it down. Once you pile on conflicting requirements, the model tries to satisfy everything at once. Instead of solving the problem, it starts juggling constraints. The result? Bloated, overengineered code that sometimes contradicts itself. The tighter the cage, the higher the risk the model starts “hallucinating” just to square the circle.

A good spec isn’t a laundry list of obsessive rules. It’s clean structure: a clear set of requirements, example inputs and outputs, explicit constraints. That gives direction without choking off the solution space.

A bad spec is 25 micro-style rules, seven mandatory design patterns, and constraints that don’t even agree with each other. At that point, the model isn’t writing code — it’s negotiating internal chaos.

Ironically, coding isn’t going away. It’s just changing shape. Instead of wrestling with the compiler, we’ll be designing the decision space for the model. Fewer lines of code. More thinking about the structure of the problem.
